The Evolution of Encryption

Humanity's fascination with secrecy dates back centuries — from Julius Caesar’s legendary cipher to the advanced encryption systems of the digital age. Over time, many devised their own ingenious methods of hiding information.

Anyone who has explored cryptography or electronics would recognize the name Enigma. This project is a tribute to that iconic machine — a marvel that once caused immense frustration for Allied cryptanalysts during World War II.

While most early ciphers relied on manual effort, one device revolutionized the field: the Enigma Machine. Developed by Nazi Germany, it employed a complex, dynamic encryption scheme using electromechanical rotors. It allowed secret messages to be encoded and decoded in ways never seen before.

Today, with powerful computational tools at our fingertips, we aim to recreate the brilliance of Enigma — not with wood and gears, but with code and creativity.
